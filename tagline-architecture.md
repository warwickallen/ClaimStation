# ClaimStation: Three-Tier Tagline Architecture

**Brand:** ClaimStation  
**Product:** Reimbursement tracking app  
**Markets:** International (English, German, Dutch, Spanish, Chinese)

---

## The Architecture

### **Tier 1: Brand Tagline** (Emotional Hook)
> **"Reclaim what's yours"**

- **Purpose:** Memorable brand essence, emotional connection
- **Length:** 3 words (perfect)
- **Tone:** Empowering, action-oriented
- **Key feature:** Clever wordplay on "reclaim/claim"
- **Use:** Hero sections, social media bios, advertising

### **Tier 2: Explanatory Tagline** (Value Proposition)
> **"All your claims, one station"**

- **Purpose:** Clear functional benefit, brand connection
- **Length:** 5 words (concise)
- **Tone:** Professional, helpful
- **Key feature:** Ties to "ClaimStation" brand name
- **Use:** Subtitles, landing pages, app stores

### **Tier 3: Supporting Copy** (Specific Details)
> **"Track health insurance, work expenses, and government reimbursements in one simple app."**

- **Purpose:** Concrete use cases and how it works
- **Length:** Full sentence
- **Tone:** Descriptive, accessible
- **Key feature:** Addresses all three target segments
- **Use:** Homepage body, descriptions, onboarding

---

## Why This Works

**Progressive clarity:**
1. **Hook** â†’ Emotional connection and curiosity
2. **Clarify** â†’ What the product does

## Voice & Tone Guidelines

**Tier 1 ("Reclaim what's yours"):**
- Voice: Bold, empowering, confident
- Tone: Active, declarative
- Emotion: Justice, ownership, control

**Tier 2 ("All your claims, one station"):**
- Voice: Clear, helpful, professional
- Tone: Explanatory, friendly
- Emotion: Relief, simplicity, trust

**Tier 3 (Supporting copy):**
- Voice: Informative, accessible, specific
- Tone: Descriptive, practical
- Emotion: Understanding, capability
